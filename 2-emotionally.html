The Oxford happiness questionnaire

Two researchers from Oxford Brookes University, Peter Hills and Michael Argyle, created in 2002 a questionnaire to measure psychological well-being: “The Oxford happiness questionnaire “. It is a tool that can help you measure your own sense of happiness therefore there are no wrong answers.
This questionnaire consists of 29 statements on happiness. These statements are either positive or negative. For each statement, there are 6 answer options.
Stephen Wright, a researcher Georgetown University’s Brain and Language Lab, gave tips on how to calculate and interpret results from the “The Oxford happiness questionnaire “.
References : Hills, P., & Argyle, M. (2002). The Oxford Happiness Questionnaire: A compact scale for the measurement of psychological well-being. Personality and Individual Differences, 33(7), 1071–1082
